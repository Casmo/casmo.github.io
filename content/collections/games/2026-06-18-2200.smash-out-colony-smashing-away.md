I needed to take a break after playing for one hour. My fingers are not used at clicking that intensifly. And for good reasons, the game was very addictive and wanted me to keep going, keep pushing.

In Smash Out Colony is a new take on the Brick Breaker genre where you swing your paddle (hammer) towards incoming projectiles. You can aim and time your hits to incoming hordes of insects. Each with different stats and abilities while collecting points to invest in upgrades. Some permanent, some will reset when game over.

**Game flow**
Smash Out Colony does a good job in building up momentum and having game beats at the right time. Each level is a quick 30-300 second run. Pacing feels smooth from starting slow and building up fast. There is enough the focus on like making hit combo's and planning ahead to where the hit the hordes of enemies. There are also moments that levels feel really overwhelming and make you stress all the way to the end. This is good and adds nicely towards the _interest curve_.

**Art style**
The style of art is cartoon with bright colors and a good mix of colors. The UI is clear and not in the way of the game play while still providing enough information in times needed. The only downside is the support for widescreen monitors.

**What I would improve**
* The UI doesn't work on widescreens while the game itself does.
* The flow when reaching the end is unclear. After 'two blueprints' (that is a set of levels with a boss on the end) you 'win' the game while you still can continue on higher difficulties. I would not limit it to two blueprints.
* Unlocking new hammers or projectiles can only be done in the main menu. This was too far away for me and I haven't used it. I'm sitting with 10k coins that I didn't know.
