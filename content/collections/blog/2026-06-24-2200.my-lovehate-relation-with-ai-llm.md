Do you remember when the GPT came out to the public? Besides the strange name it did something most of us never experienced before, a chatbot that gave tailored replies to users input. Not the standard replies that 'the old chat bots' where spitting out. No, GPT gave real answers that felt awkwardly human.
If you are like me, you probably tried to make a chatbot before, and all it did was trying to make a standard back and forth conversation with an occasional scout for keywords to make it 'less' obvious. Or, making a endless tree of conversation branches with standard questions and answers.

But GPT was something different. It "understood" you. It answered like a human. It was surely not sentient, but I'm sure, you asked a similar question too. And I was giving it questions or tasks that I was sure nobody tried before. And it did what I asked. Weird. I loved it.

Now, years later, and the world changed and is still changing. At least digitally for sure. Everyone is talking about it, everybody is using it (freely or forced) and everyone has an opinion about it. And for good reason. News about environment impact, billion dollar investments, jobs its creating, jobs it is killing, diseases it found cures for, people it have killed in war, disruption it makes in electronic devices market, etc, etc. It cannot be ignored without having some sort opinion about it. And I hate most of it.

With time passing most of us know more and more about it. The underlying technique is better understood and in the implications become, sometimes, more clear. It is a massive powerful tool that have real impact in real situations. I'm an engineer and I have been developing for almost 30 years. And this tool, for now, is changing my workflow more than any previous tool in the field. From notepad to Dreamweaver from powerful IDE's to maturing programming languages from Frameworks and (open source) tools to all software that the world runs on. The AI, well, the LLM, is writing code faster than humanly possible. And, sometimes, with great accuracy. I sometimes love that.

So. Let's focus on the LLM part in the industry I'm most knowledgeable of. Engineering. The development side. Especially in games, apps and websites. I cannot think that I would ever remove this tool in my workflows again. Not until something better flies by or when this technique is destroying itself. I'm finally mastering this 'language' and besides heavily reducing tedious work for myself I'm creating the tools and workflows for others to help them where the focus always was: putting the users first and letting the systems work for them, not the other way around. I love that.

Not to end with a negative feeling but one thing should be clear. We do not need massive amounts of generated AI shit, we all know it, the AI slop. Stop abusing this tool to spam. We always had spam and we always fought it. This time it is no different. This time it is even more important. Use the tool for good, responsibly. Don't jack into human vulnerabilities and feed it garbage. Nobody wants to forcefully get in touch with it. Enable it to make tedious work obsolete while still enabling human creativity. I'm carefully positive.
